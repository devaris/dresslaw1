Questions and answers

Test name: Low calorie sweeteners

1. What are low / no calorie sweeteners?

Food additives with intense sweet taste that provide few or no calories.

2. Are low / no calorie sweeteners safe?

Yes, LNCS as all food additives are approved ONLY after undergoing a strict safety evaluation by food safety authorities.

3. Can LNCS really help with weight management?

Yes, they can help manage weight if used consistently to reduce calorie intake as part of an overall sensible, balanced diet, combined with regular physical activity.

4. Are low / no calorie sweeteners safe for all populations?

Yes, LNCS are safe to be consumed by all populations. Aspartame cannot be consumed by people suffering from the rare genetic disorder PKU (Phenylketonuria).

5. So, if LNCSS Are safe, why do we keep on reading so many negatives about them?

Unfounded claims that something is unsafe travels faster and is deemed more interesting to publish. Health authorities all over the world assure us that LNCS are safe.

6. Is aspartame safe?

Yes. Hundreds of studies have been conducted to support the safety of aspartame. It is one of the most thoroughly researched and studied food ingredients in use today.

Test name: Artificiality

1. How can something 'artificial' be good for me to eat or drink?

“Artificial” does not necessarily mean “bad” and “natural” does not necessarily mean “good”!

2. What are food additives?

Additives are substances that are added in foodstuff to perform a specific technological function.

3. What does the 'E' in front of some Coca-Cola ingredients mean?

E-numbers are a systematic way of “coding” different food additives in the European Union (EU).

4. How many additives does Coca-Cola have?

2

5. How can it be that Coca-Cola has no added preservatives?

The unique combination of ingredients is responsible for the stability of Coca-Cola.

6. Are food additives safe?

All authorized food additives, including the ones in our products, are safe.

7. I have heard that some E numbers are more dangerous than others.

There is NO SUCH THING as good or bad, safe or dangerous E numbers. They are all safe when used at levels specified by authorities.

Test name: Energy balance and active lifestyle

1. What is energy balance?

It is the balance between the calories you take in from foods and drinks and the calories your body burns to carry out your daily activities including physical activity (walking, running, etc.), standing, breathing, maintenance of body temperature, and cell and organ function.

2. How many calories are there in a 330ml can of COCA-COLA?

139

3. How can Coca-Cola be part of a balanced, sensible diet?

All foods and beverages can have a place in a sensible, balanced diet combined with regular physical activity. But it’s important to remember that all calories count, no matter what food or beverage they come from, including those from Coca-Cola.

4. Isn't it drinks like Coca-Cola that make people fat?

Coca-Cola, just like many other foods or beverages contains calories. Obesity and overweight are mainly caused by the imbalance of energy: too many calories consumed versus too few calories burned off.

5. Which calories are responsible for gaining weight?

When it comes to weight management, all calories derived from protein, carbohydrates (including sugars,) fat and alcohol count. You will gain weight if you consume more calories than you "burn".

6. If I do drink a 250ml glass of Coca-Cola how much of my guideline daily amount of sugars WILL I have used up?

29% to be precise.

Test name: Ingredients

1. How much water is there in COCA-COLA?

>85%

2. Do sugar-sweetened beverages cause diabetes?

Consuming too many calories, from any combination of food and beverages, without burning enough calories to keep your energy balance, can lead to overweight and obesity, which are risk factors for diabetes.

3. Isn't it true that the carbonation causes indigestion?

No. While carbonation causes a temporary feeling of fullness, it does not cause indigestion.

4. Isn't it true that the carbonation creates cellulite?

Carbonated drinks have not been shown to cause cellulite.

5. Isn’t it true that Coca-Cola shouldn’t be given to children because it makes them hyperactive due to the sugar?

No, sugar is not to blame for children hyperactivity.

6. Since Coca-Cola has caffeine, how can it contribute to my hydration?

All beverages hydrate, even caffeinated ones.

7. How much caffeine is in a 250ml serving of Coca-Cola?

The amount of caffeine in Coca-Cola is less than 1/3 of that in the same amount of brewed coffee.

8. Isn't Coca-Cola responsible for tooth decay?

If proper dental hygiene is not practiced, any food or beverage that contains sugars (fermentable carbohydrates) can play a role in the development of tooth decay.

9. If there is nothing wrong with the Coca-Cola formula, why is it such a top secret?

The unique blend of natural flavors is what makes Coca-Cola taste unique. It has been a tradition for more than 125 years to keep the Coca-Cola formula a well-guarded secret, and we’re not going to change that now!

10. Doesn't phosphoric acid in drinks like Coca-Cola cause osteoporosis?

Osteoporosis can be caused by numerous factors, but phosphoric acid is not one of them.
